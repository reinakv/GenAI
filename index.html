What does AI-generated music mean? To understand where we are today, let's travel back in time a bit....

11th to 14th century

Music was primarily preserved in monasteries and cathedrals, with limited distribution. People who wished to enjoy music often visited these establishments to listen and partake. Manuscript collections, such as the Chantilly Codex and the Montpellier Codex, preserved a wealth of secular and sacred music from this period.

15th century

Johannes Gutenberg invented the very first printer. This invention revolutionized nearly every aspect of human life. In the context of music, people were able to share music sheets and ideas for composition for the first time.

16th-17th Century

The Renaissance period, characterized by a revival of interest in classical art, literature, and learning, as well as a shift towards humanism, individualism, and scientific inquiry. Music printing flourished during this era, with the publication of choral collections, instrumental treatises, and theoretical works by prominent composers and music theorists.

18th-19th Century

During the Enlightenment era, paralleling the Industrial Revolution, music became more about expression and emotions. Music transitioned from being a rarity to a luxury with the introduction of opera halls and public concerts. These venues provided dedicated spaces for the performance and dissemination of music to wider audiences, democratizing access to music and culture.

20th Century

The Digital Revolution once again took the world by storm, making music something that could be enjoyed in one's own space without any restrictions. The incorporation of music into film and television productions became increasingly important, influencing popular culture and broadening the audience for various musical genres.

Now

Streaming platforms have become the dominant mode of music consumption, with users accessing vast libraries of music on-demand through subscription services or ad-supported platforms.

We transitioned from a time when music was seldom heard and viewed as something to be conserved, to an era where it's uncommon not to engage with music in some capacity every single day. Throughout history, such changes have prompted humans to adapt and evolve, striving for greater efficiency and quality in the way we live.

With the advent of each new era, concerns have often arisen regarding the potential scarcity of employment opportunities. Yet history attests otherwise. With each passing era, transformations catalyze industries with fresh ideas and innovations, invariably leading to expansion and improvement.
